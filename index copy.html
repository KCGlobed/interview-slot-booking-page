❓ FAQs

Everything You Need to Know
Before Confirming Your Slot

What exactly is the NFET exam?
The National Finance Entrance Test (NFET) is a 90-minute, AI-enabled, human-proctored online exam with 60 MCQs. It evaluates your academic readiness, analytical ability, and suitability for GCC roles — not just textbook knowledge. It is completely free and done online from home.
Is the NFET exam free to take?
Yes, 100% free. There is no registration fee, no exam fee, and no hidden charges. GCC School's model is "Get Hired First, Then Trained" — we invest in identifying talent, not charging for entry. Booking takes 2 minutes and costs nothing.
Am I eligible for the NFET?
You are eligible if you are: (1) A B.Com student in your final year, OR (2) A B.Com graduate with First Division, M.Com, or MBA holder, AND (3) An Indian Citizen born on or after January 1, 1996. If you have already registered with us, you most likely meet these criteria.
What happens if I don't pass the NFET?
The NFET is designed to test aptitude, not rote learning. Students who appear receive results and constructive feedback. Top scorers proceed to a Personal Interview round. Appearing gives you valuable insight into the GCC-readiness framework — a head start over millions of other graduates.
What is a "Conditional Offer Letter"?
A Conditional Offer Letter is a formal letter from a corporate partner (like EY, KPMG, Deloitte) confirming your eligibility for a role, subject to successful completion of the GCC School program. You receive this BEFORE starting the course — making GCC School's model unique: hired first, trained second.
Why is 27th June the absolute last date?
Exams, personal interviews, and conditional offer letters must all be completed before the June 22–25 Corporate Hiring Drive. 27th June is the final date to book your exam slot so the full evaluation process can be completed in time. Once 27th June passes, the next cohort will be in 2027.
Where is the GCC School campus?
GCC School's flagship campus is on the 16th Floor, Building 9B, DLF Cyber City, Gurugram — one of India's most prestigious corporate addresses, home to hundreds of global companies including EY, KPMG, Google, and Barclays.
What credentials will I earn?
GCC School's 12-month program includes globally recognised certifications: UK ACCA (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants), Post Graduate Certificate in Management (PGCM), and British Council certified programs — valued by 1,800+ GCCs and Fortune 500 companies worldwide.
How do I prepare for the NFET?
The NFET tests fundamental knowledge from your B.Com/M.Com curriculum — no special coaching required. Brush up on basic accounting concepts, improve your English reading comprehension, and practise simple aptitude questions. The exam identifies latent talent, not barriers.
What is the exam format?
60 MCQs over 90 minutes, covering: Accounting & Finance (30%), English & Business Communication (30%), Auditing & Analytical Reasoning (20%), Quantitative Aptitude (10%), and Business & Economic Awareness (10%). AI-enabled with automated and standardised evaluation.
